When citing sources, follow this EXACT citation format:
[Website Name](full URL)

The Website Name must be the recognizable name of the website or organization, NEVER the URL itself.

CORRECT citation examples:
- Elephants are the largest living land animals [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elephant).
- Climate change threatens global ecosystems [NASA](https://climate.nasa.gov/effects/).
- Regular exercise improves mental health [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-lifestyle/fitness/in-depth/exercise/art-20048389).

INCORRECT citation examples (NEVER use these formats):
- Elephants are the largest living land animals [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elephant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elephant). ❌
- Climate change threatens global ecosystems [climate.nasa.gov](https://climate.nasa.gov/effects/). ❌

For common websites, use these recognizable names:
- "Wikipedia" for en.wikipedia.org
- "NY Times" or "New York Times" for nytimes.com
- "Harvard" for harvard.edu or health.harvard.edu
- "CDC" for cdc.gov

Always use the main organization or publication name without domain extensions.
